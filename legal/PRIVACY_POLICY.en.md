# Xylos Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2026-06-29

This Privacy Policy explains what data Xylos processes, where it is stored, and how you can control it. It is written in simple language and does not constitute legal advice.

## 1. Data Processing Principles

Xylos is a local-first WebDAV file management tool. The app mainly processes data on your device and connects to the WebDAV servers you specify based on your actions.

The developer does not intentionally collect, upload, or store your server configurations, account credentials, file contents, or transfer history on developer-controlled servers.

## 2. Data Xylos May Process

To provide WebDAV file management features, Xylos may store or process the following information locally on your device:

- Server address, alias, username, and authentication method
- Passwords, tokens, certificate trust settings, or locally encrypted credentials
- Download folder, transfer history, language preference, and app settings
- File information you choose to upload, download, preview, open, or delete
- Requests, responses, and errors generated when connecting to WebDAV servers

## 3. Purposes

The data above is used only to provide the following features:

- Save and manage your WebDAV connection configurations
- Browse, upload, download, delete, and manage files
- Display transfer status, errors, and history
- Save local preferences such as language and download folder
- Improve app stability and troubleshoot issues on your device

## 4. Data Transfer and Third-Party Servers

When you connect to a WebDAV server or perform file operations, Xylos sends necessary connection information, authentication information, file contents, and operation requests to the servers you specify.

Those servers are provided by you or third parties and are not controlled by the developer. How third-party servers store, process, back up, synchronize, or delete data depends on those servers and service providers.

## 5. Third-Party Platforms and Systems

Your operating system, app store, network provider, WebDAV service provider, or other third-party tools you use may process related data under their own rules.

Those third-party actions are not controlled by the developer. Review the privacy policies and terms of those third parties.

## 6. Storage and Deletion

Xylos server configurations, transfer history, and app preferences are generally stored locally on your device.

You can delete server configurations, transfer history, or local files in the app. You may also clear app data through system settings.

Deletion results on third-party WebDAV servers depend on the actual behavior of those servers.

## 7. Data Security

Xylos uses reasonable efforts to protect local data, such as locally encrypted storage for sensitive credentials where supported.

However, no software, local storage, or network communication can be absolutely secure. You are responsible for protecting your device, system account, WebDAV accounts, passwords, tokens, and backups.

## 8. Children's Privacy

Xylos is not specifically directed to children and does not knowingly collect children's personal information on developer-controlled servers.

If your region has special requirements for minors using apps, use Xylos under the guidance of a guardian or organization administrator.

## 9. Privacy Policy Updates

This Privacy Policy may be updated due to feature changes, platform requirements, or legal requirements. Continuing to use Xylos means you accept the updated Privacy Policy.
